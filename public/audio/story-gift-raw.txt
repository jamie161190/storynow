My darling Livvie, this story is just for you, from Grandma, because you are the most wonderful little dancer I have ever seen. I hope it makes you smile as big as your hugs. All my love, always.

... ...

Once upon a very quiet morning, a little girl named Livvie was dancing in her bedroom.

She was dancing with Mr Elephant, who was an excellent partner and never complained when Livvie changed the music halfway through. She spun, she twirled, she leapt as high as the top of the bed ... and then, with the greatest dignity you have ever seen in someone wearing pyjamas, Livvie took a deep bow.

Mr Elephant seemed very impressed.

[sighs] Outside the window, a butterfly watched from the garden. It was the colour of sunshine and peach blossom, and it had been watching Livvie dance for a very long time.

When Livvie spotted it, she pressed her nose to the glass.

[whispers] "Hello," she said.

The butterfly fluttered once, twice ... and then, to Livvie's absolute amazement, it beckoned with one wing.

She pulled on her shoes, tucked Mr Elephant carefully under her arm, and followed.

The butterfly led her through the garden, through the little gate at the bottom, and into a place Livvie had never seen before. A meadow. But not just any meadow ... a secret butterfly meadow, where the air was thick and soft and full of colour, and butterflies of every shade you could imagine drifted like living flowers through the long grass.

Livvie stopped walking.

[gasps] There were hundreds of them. Blue ones and gold ones and ones the colour of deep summer sky. Some were small as fingernails. Some were wide as open books. And all around her, the meadow hummed with a sound that was almost, almost like music.

Livvie did what Livvie always did when there was music.

She danced.

She danced through the meadow with Mr Elephant held high, and the butterflies swirled around her like a living, breathing rainbow. She leapt and she spun and she dipped ... and when the music in the air reached its most beautiful moment, Livvie came to a stop, and she took her bow.

A perfect, deep, very serious bow.

The butterflies rose in a great shimmering cloud and filled the sky above her.

... ...

And then a hand reached down and took hers.

Livvie looked up.

[whispers] It was Grandma.

Grandma was there in the meadow, and she was smiling the smile she always saved just for Livvie. "I wondered if you'd find this place," Grandma said softly. "I found it a very long time ago. I always hoped you would come here one day."

Livvie took Grandma's hand properly in both of hers. "Did you dance here too?" she asked.

"Every chance I got," said Grandma. "And I always bowed at the end."

[laughs softly] Livvie beamed. "I do that too. Even in the supermarket."

"I know," said Grandma, and her eyes went very bright and warm. "I have seen you. And every single time, Livvie, it is the most wonderful thing."

They sat together in the long grass for a while, Grandma and Livvie and Mr Elephant, while the butterflies drifted and the meadow hummed. Livvie leaned her head against Grandma's arm and felt the warmth of it, the particular warmth that only Grandma had, like sunshine and home all at once.

[sighs] "Will we be able to come back?" Livvie asked quietly.

Grandma squeezed her hand. "Whenever you dance with your whole heart," she said, "this meadow is always here."

... ...

When Livvie found herself back in her bedroom, Mr Elephant was still tucked under her arm and the morning light was coming soft through the curtains. She stood in the middle of the floor for a moment.

Then she danced, just a few steps, just for the joy of it.

And at the end, she bowed.

[whispers] Somewhere, in a meadow full of colour and light, the butterflies rose.

This story was made just for Livvie.
